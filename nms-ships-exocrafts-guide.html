Section 01

Ship Classes

Every ship in No Man's Sky belongs to one of nine types, each with distinct stats, bonuses, and visual silhouettes. Choosing the right type for your playstyle is the most impactful equipment decision in the game.

🚀 Fighter Combat Specialist

Highest damage output and shields. Ideal for space combat, piracy, and sentinel battles. Agile and fast.

🔭 Explorer Hyperdrive Master

Best hyperdrive range and scanner bonuses. Built for reaching distant systems and surveying planets.

📦 Hauler Cargo King

Largest inventory capacity (up to 120 slots) and best shields. Slow and wide — the trading workhorse.

🛸 Shuttle Jack of All Trades

Balanced stats across the board. A solid starter ship, but outclassed by specialists at higher tiers.

⚡ Exotic Rare All-Rounder

Always S-class, always unique. Near-maxed stats in all categories. Extremely rare — buy on sight.

☀ Solar Ship Vesper Sail

Sleek ships with a distinctive Vesper Sail. Found mostly in Outlaw systems (85% spawn rate there). Balanced explorer-type stats.

🌐 Interceptor Salvaged Sentinel

Hijacked Sentinel ships. Hover in place, equipped with anti-grav. Recovered from crashed wrecks on Dissonant planets only.

🛠 Corvette Custom-Built

Large, fully customizable ships built piece-by-piece at Corvette Workshops on Space Stations. Voyagers update (2025).

🌿 Living Ship Organic Vessel

A biological starship grown, not built. Uses unique organic upgrade organs upgraded via Psychonic Eggs. Always S-class with 36 Cargo + 30 Tech slots.

Class Rating System

Each ship type also has a quality class — C, B, A, or S — that determines its base stats, inventory size, and technology slot count. S-class is always best. Max inventory varies by ship type (Haulers reach 120 cargo slots; Fighters top out around 100), but within each type, S-class gives the maximum.

Finding Ships

Ships are found through several methods. Knowing which method targets which class and type gives you control over what you collect rather than leaving it to chance.

Discovery Methods

🛸
Space Station Passive Landing

Ships land regularly at the Space Station hangar. Inspect every ship that docks — check class and stats by approaching and holding the interaction prompt. Best for finding random deals without flying anywhere.

All types Random class Free to wait
🪐
Trade Posts Planet Surface

Planetary Trade Posts see a constant stream of landing ships. Higher traffic than stations and can spawn rarer designs. Use a Signal Scanner or Planetary Chart to locate them.

High volume Any class Best for S-class
📡
Crashed Ships Salvage Recovery

Crashed ships are found via Signal Scanner (select Distress Signal) or Planetary Charts. Free to claim but need repairs. Usually C or B class — but can sometimes yield rare designs.

Free Needs repair C/B class mainly
🌌
Exotic Hunting System Camping

Exotic ships appear in specific system types. Camp at a high-traffic Trade Post and reload saves to reroll landing ships. Coordinate databases (r/NMSCoordinateExchange) list systems with rare Exotic spawns.

Always S-class Patience required Highest value

Save Scumming for S-Class

1
Find an active Trade Post — land and watch ships arrive. Note the landing frequency.
2
Manual save — press the save button (not autosave). This locks your position but rerolls the landing queue.
3
Wait and inspect — check each ship that lands. If nothing good arrives after 5–6 ships, reload the save.
4
Repeat — each reload rerolls the ship queue. An S-class of your chosen type can appear within a few reloads.
Trade-In Value

When buying a new ship, you can trade in your current ship for a credit discount on the new one. Always trade in your oldest/lowest-value ship. The trade-in value is based on the ship's class and slot count.

Buying on Discount

Ships can sometimes be purchased at a discount from NPCs in these situations:

  • NPCs in Space Stations occasionally offer their personal ship for sale at 10–30% below market rate
  • Freighter battles: the defending freighter's captain sometimes offers their escort fighter at a discount after you save them
  • Settlement Hauler Landing Pad: ships that land at your settlement can be purchased at a discount
  • Space Anomaly: the Iteration: Helios sometimes marks ships down for travellers

Fabricator & Corvettes

No Man's Sky offers two custom ship-building systems. The Starship Fabricator on Space Stations (Orbital update) lets you design and build Fighter, Explorer, Hauler, Shuttle, and Solar Ships from procedural parts. The Corvette Workshop (Voyagers update, 2025) lets you build large, fully-modular Corvette-class ships piece by piece.

The Starship Fabricator

1
Visit any Space Station — the Starship Fabricator terminal is located near the technology vendors.
2
Choose ship type — Fighter, Explorer, Hauler, Shuttle, or Solar. This determines the base part catalogue available.
3
Unlock parts with Nanites — each component (cockpit, wing, thruster, body, etc.) must be purchased first. Parts cost ~50–800 nanites each.
4
Assemble your ship — mix and match unlocked parts freely. Preview updates in real time. Different manufacturer parts create unique visual hybrids.
5
Paint and detail — choose primary, secondary, and accent colors, plus metallic finishes and decal options.
6
Fabricate and pay — the final cost is in Units, scaling with part count and complexity. Class is rolled randomly on fabrication — save before pressing confirm and reload until you get S-class.

Part Categories

🧠 Cockpit Visual · Core

Defines the front profile and pilot viewport style. One of the most recognizable visual elements.

🦅 Wings Visual · Lift

Varies from swept-back fighter wings to heavy cargo brackets. Dramatically changes silhouette.

🔥 Thrusters Visual · Propulsion

Engine pod design. Some glow with plasma effect. Some are bulky industrial, others are sleek racing units.

🛡 Body / Hull Visual · Frame

The central fuselage. Determines overall mass and silhouette. Mixes with wings for thousands of combos.

🔫 Nose / Weapon Visual · Front

Front-mounted cannons, sensors, or decorative prows. Fighters have the most aggressive options.

🎨 Paint Scheme Visual · Color

Full color picker for primary, secondary, and accent zones. Metallic, matte, or pearlescent finishes.

Corvette Workshop (Voyagers Update)

The Corvette Workshop is a separate ship-building system on Space Stations, accessed via the warp points to the Corvette landing pads. Corvette-class ships are large, fully modular vessels with full furnished interiors that can carry passengers.

1
Find the Corvette Workshop — walk through the warp gates on the left side of any Space Station to reach the dedicated Corvette landing pads, then interact with the workshop console.
2
Build with snap-together modules — habitation modules, hulls, wings, engines, weapons, reactors, shield generators, and decorative pieces all snap to each other in a build editor.
3
Choose your hab style — three distinct aesthetics available: Titan, Thunderbird, and Ambassador, each with its own interior furniture style.
4
Furnish the interior — inside the parked Corvette, open the build menu to add beds, refiners, cargo containers, windows, and any base-building parts.
5
Find more modules — basic modules are bought at the Corvette Workshop. The full catalogue is found in Salvage Containers on planets with Salvageable Scrap listed as a resource, or traded for at the Workshop.
Corvette Combat Stats

Corvettes use standard ship technology modules in addition to whatever stat bonuses their built-in modules provide. Functionally they're a hybrid of standard ship and freighter mechanics — and they can hover, eject players to spacewalk, and ferry passengers between systems.

S-Class Fabrication Strategy

For the Starship Fabricator: build your ideal ship visually, then save before fabricating. Check the resulting class — if it's not S, reload and fabricate again. The class is rolled at the moment of fabrication. Average reloads for S-class: 3–8.

Sentinel Interceptors

Sentinel Interceptors are salvaged Sentinel ships rebuilt for player use. They're found exclusively on Dissonant planets in Dissonant systems. They have unique tech (Anti-Gravity Well, Crimson Core) and can hover mid-air.

1
Find a Dissonant system — open the Galaxy Map and look for systems labelled "Dissonant" instead of an element name. Only one planet per system has Dissonance, marked with "Dissonance detected" in the scan.
2
Get an Echo Locator — destroy Dissonance Resonators (tall drilling Sentinels guarded by Corrupted Sentinels) on the planet. They drop Echo Locators (and sometimes Inverted Mirrors).
3
Use the Echo Locator from your inventory — it marks a nearby Harmonic Camp. Travel there and find the Harmonic Interface terminal.
4
Solve the glyph puzzle — the terminal shows three math problems. Calculate them, then input the answers as Override Glyphs. Choose "Locate Dissonance Spikes" to mark a crashed Interceptor.
5
Travel to the crash site — interact with the wreck, gather the Hyaline Brain. Use "Probe Subconscious" to find an Ancient Site, then cleanse the brain at the Monolith → Harmonic Brain.
6
Repair the Pilot Interface — using Radiant Shards (from terrain), Inverted Mirrors, and the Harmonic Brain. The Interceptor becomes yours.
Interceptor Stat Reroll

Each system has a single Interceptor design but class is rolled per crash site. Save manually before interacting with the wreck — if the stats are bad, reload and the class rerolls without forcing you to redo the puzzle. You can also farm multiple wrecks from the same Harmonic Camp by leaving and using the Spike Locator again.

Carrier AI Fragment Alternative

Destroy a Sentinel Capital Ship (spawns at max wanted level in space combat) to receive a Carrier AI Fragment. Activate it to be directed to a crashed Interceptor in any Dissonant system. Slower than the Echo Locator method but doesn't require puzzle-solving.

Living Ships

Living Ships are biological starships — grown, not manufactured. They are fundamentally different from regular ships: no standard technology modules can be installed, and they use unique organic upgrade systems.

Obtaining a Living Ship

1
Buy a Void Egg — Purchase from the Quicksilver Synthesis Companion on the Space Anomaly for 3,200 Quicksilver. Earn Quicksilver from daily Nexus missions and weekend community events.
2
Pulse Drive with the Egg — Keep the Void Egg in your active starship inventory and engage Pulse Drive in space (not warp). After a short while, you'll receive a strange transmission and an alien Living Ship will appear, kicking off the Starbirth quest.
3
Complete the Starbirth chain — The egg gives you cryptic clues leading to planets in different star systems. You'll gather and craft organic items over several real-time days as the egg matures through timed stages.
4
Claim your Living Ship — At the end of the chain, you'll be directed to a crash site. Living Ships are always S-class with 36 Cargo + 30 Tech slots. Subsequent Living Ships require another Void Egg + 10,000 Nanites.

Living Ship Upgrade Organs

Living Ship "organs" are the equivalent of normal ship technology, but use unique organic components and recipes. They are obtained from Void Egg space encounters and from frigate expeditions with Organic Frigates.

👁 Grafted Eyes Weapon (Phase Beam)

The Living Ship's beam weapon. Drains shields efficiently. Recharged with rare organic materials.

🧠 Neural Assembly Hyperdrive Analogue

The hyperdrive equivalent. Can warp to red, green, and blue stars without needing Cadmium/Emeril/Indium drives.

🫀 Pulsing Heart Pulse Engine

Powers in-system travel. Faster pulse jumps than standard ships when fully upgraded.

🦠 Scream Suppressor Shield

The Living Ship's deflector shield. Regenerates organically without manual recharging.

🌀 Spewing Vents Weapon (Photon Cannon)

The primary weapon. Equivalent of a Photon Cannon. Living Ships only have these two weapon types.

🥚 Psychonic Eggs Upgrade Modules

Used to upgrade organs to higher classes. Obtained from space encounters and Organic Frigate missions.

Living Ship Advantages

Living Ships warp to all colored star types without needing the corresponding fuel drives, regenerate shields passively over time, and have unique weapon visuals. The trade-off: only two built-in weapon types (Spewing Vents and Grafted Eyes), no other weapons available, and they cannot be scrapped — only traded for another ship.

Ship Upgrades

Ship upgrades are technology modules installed in the dedicated Technology grid. Stacking same-category modules with adjacency bonuses is the key to maximum ship performance.

Core Upgrade Categories

⚡ Pulse Engine Propulsion

Boosts atmospheric speed, boost strength, and pulse drive efficiency. Stack 3 for max planetary travel speed.

🌌 Hyperdrive Warp Range

Increases warp range per jump. Unlock Emeril, Cadmium, and Indium drives for colored star access.

🔫 Photon Cannon Weapons

Primary space weapon. Upgrades increase damage, fire rate, and heat dissipation. Best general-purpose weapon.

💥 Phase Beam Weapons

Laser that drains enemy shields fast. Best for sustained fights. Synergizes well with shield upgrades.

🚀 Rocket Launcher Weapons

High burst damage. Ideal for quickly destroying large targets. Limited ammo — use strategically.

🛡 Deflector Shield Defense

Increases shield strength and regeneration rate. Stack 3 adjacent to each other for maximum protection.

📡 Scanning Array Survey

Increases planetary scan range and reveals resource deposits from orbit. Essential for Explorers.

🧲 Launch Thruster Takeoff

Reduces Launch Thruster fuel cost per takeoff. Fully upgraded can reduce cost by up to 75%. Always worth installing.

Adjacency Bonus Rules

Critical Mechanic

Placing same-category upgrade modules directly adjacent to each other (horizontally or vertically, not diagonally) grants a bonus to all connected modules. A cluster of 3 adjacent S-class Hyperdrive modules massively outperforms 3 non-adjacent ones. Always plan your technology grid layout before installing.

  • Up to 3 upgrade modules of the same type can be installed per ship
  • The base technology (e.g. Hyperdrive) must be installed before upgrade modules
  • Upgrade modules should always be adjacent to the base technology AND each other
  • S-class upgrades give the highest per-module bonus — always worth the nanite investment
  • Use the Technology inventory grid's visual layout to plan adjacency clusters before purchasing

Recommended Priority Order

1
Launch Thruster upgrades — Reduce the ongoing cost of takeoffs. The QoL payoff is immediate.
2
Hyperdrive + colored star drives — Open up the full galaxy. Cadmium, Emeril, and Indium drives unlock red, green, and blue stars.
3
Pulse Engine ×3 — Dramatically improves how fast and fun planetary flight feels.
4
Weapons ×3 (your primary) — Choose your main weapon and max it out before adding secondary weapons.
5
Shield ×3 — Survival during combat becomes dramatically easier with a fully-upgraded Deflector Shield.

Multi-Tools

The Multi-Tool is your handheld device for mining, scanning, and combat. Like ships, they come in multiple types and classes with significant stat differences.

Multi-Tool Types

🔫 Pistol Balanced

Compact and portable. Good mining and decent combat. Best starter type. Widely available.

🪖 Rifle Combat Focus

High damage output. More tech slots than pistol. Best for sentinel fighting builds.

⚗ Experimental Scanner Specialist

Highest scan bonuses. Best for discovering flora and fauna for nanite income.

🌿 Alien Mining Specialist

Best mining bonuses. Top choice for efficient resource gathering. Harder to find.

🔮 Royal Premium

Rare, beautiful designs with excellent balanced stats. Found in specific systems by following bird-like creatures to Royal cabinets.

🤖 Sentinel Combat / Damage

Recovered from cabinets at Harmonic Camps on Dissonant planets after solving the override-glyph puzzle. High damage bonus, free but starts with damaged slots.

💎 Atlantid Crystalline Rare

Crystalline multi-tool style. Found via specific quest interactions and at certain settlements. Distinct glowing aesthetic.

Finding Multi-Tools

  • Space Station cabinets — the display case in the room past the Exosuit upgrade NPC. One tool per station.
  • Alien NPCs at stations and Trade Posts sometimes offer their personal tool for purchase
  • Minor Settlements have a Multi-Tool cabinet in the Trade Terminal building — often with rarer types
  • Harmonic Camps on Dissonant planets contain Sentinel Multi-Tools after solving the Multi-Tool seal puzzle
  • Space Anomaly — the Multi-Tool Decommissioning Terminal next to Iteration: Tethys lets you scrap old tools for nanites
  • Save-scumming works for tools too: reload before interacting with a cabinet to reroll class and stats

Key Multi-Tool Upgrades

⛏ Mining Beam Resource Gathering

Upgrade to increase mining speed and heat tolerance. Stack 3 for near-instant resource extraction.

💥 Boltcaster Combat

Primary combat weapon. Stack 3 upgrades for serious damage output against sentinels.

🌊 Scatter Blaster Close Combat

Shotgun-style close-range weapon. Devastating against single targets at short range.

🔭 Analysis Visor Scanner

Upgrades reveal more resource deposits and increase scan bonus per discovery. Key for nanite farming.

💣 Plasma Launcher Area Damage

Fires explosive rounds. Excellent for clearing groups of sentinels. Also useful for terrain manipulation.

🔬 Terrain Manipulator Excavation

Digs and places terrain. Essential for cave mining and base building. Upgrade for faster excavation.

Exocrafts

Exocrafts are ground and aquatic vehicles you can summon on planetary surfaces. Each fills a different role — from fast scouting to deep mining to underwater exploration. They're invaluable for efficient planet traversal and bulk resource gathering.

Unlocking Exocrafts

All Exocraft Geobay blueprints can be purchased at the Construction Research Station aboard the Space Anomaly for Salvaged Data (~4–10 each). Alternatively, hire an Exocraft Technician at your base from a Space Station and complete their mission line to unlock blueprints sequentially.

Summoning

Built Exocrafts are summoned at their Geobay on a planet. To summon anywhere on a planet, build an Exocraft Summoning Station at a base in the same system. To summon any Exocraft anywhere on any planet without a Geobay at all, install the Orbital Exocraft Materialiser technology on your freighter.

All Exocraft Types

🚗
Roamer All-Terrain Scout

4-wheeled balanced Exocraft. Decent speed, can mine resources, has a scanner. 28 inventory slots. The best all-rounder and usually the first one players unlock.

Balanced Mining capable 28 slots
🚜
Colossus Heavy Hauler

Massive 8-wheeled mining truck with the biggest cargo capacity of any Exocraft (40+ slots). Slow but excellent for bulk resource extraction. Comes with mining laser built in.

40+ inventory Slow but stable Built-in mining
🤖
Minotaur Combat Mech

Bipedal mech suit with heavy weapons and shields. Immune to planetary hazards — no suit power drain inside. Best for combat operations and hazardous world exploration.

Hazard immune Heavy weapons Combat focus
🏍
Pilgrim Two-Wheel Climber

Two-wheeled motorbike with the fastest top speed and longest boost when upgraded. Can climb steep hills and cliffs that other Exocraft can't. 16 inventory slots — pure travel, not cargo.

Fastest top speed Climbs cliffs 16 slots
🌊
Nautilon Submarine

Deep-water submarine with mining laser and cannon. The only Exocraft designed for underwater exploration. Uses the unique Humboldt Drive (fueled by Hydrothermal Fuel Cells). 28 slots.

Underwater only Mining + combat Humboldt Drive
🛸
Nomad Hover Scout

Lightweight hovercraft that glides over water at speed. Most fuel-efficient Exocraft. Has the Acceleration Module pre-installed. 30 inventory slots. Recommended for Portal travel and water-heavy planets.

Hovers over water Fuel efficient 30 slots

Exocraft Upgrades

Each Exocraft has its own technology grid for upgrades. Modules are specific to each vehicle — a Colossus upgrade won't fit a Roamer. Buy or craft them and install using the same adjacency bonus rules as ships.

Universal Upgrade Priorities

⛏ Mining Laser All Exocrafts

Install and upgrade on any mining-capable Exocraft. Dramatically faster than the Multi-Tool for large deposits.

🔋 Exocraft Acceleration Engine Upgrade

Boosts top speed and boost duration. Priority on Roamer and Pilgrim for traversal efficiency.

📡 Survey Device Scanner

Reveals buried resource deposits in a radius around the Exocraft. Essential for efficient mining runs.

🛡 Exocraft Shield Defense

Adds shield capacity. Priority for Minotaur to make it a proper combat platform.

🔫 Mounted Cannon Weapons

Vehicle-mounted weapons for Exocrafts. Upgrade for Minotaur to turn it into a sentinel-killing machine.

🧲 Cargo Expansion Colossus Only

Increases Colossus inventory capacity. Stack these — the Colossus's purpose is cargo, so maximize it.

Exocraft-Specific Recommendations

Orbital Exocraft Materialiser

Install the Orbital Exocraft Materialiser technology on your freighter and you can summon any Exocraft anywhere on any planet — no Geobay or Summoning Station required. One of the best quality-of-life upgrades for players who use Exocrafts regularly. Purchase the blueprint from Iteration: Hyperion on the Anomaly.

Pro Tips & Secrets

💡 Own Multiple Ships

You can own up to 12 ships simultaneously. Keep a dedicated Fighter for combat, an Explorer for long warps, a Hauler for trading runs, and your favorite for daily use. Swap between them via the Quick Menu anywhere.

💡 Exotic Ships — Always S-Class

Exotic ships are always S-class with no exceptions, and always have a unique visual design that appears nowhere else. They're smaller than standard ships but their stats punch above their size. If you see an Exotic land, buy it immediately — it may never return.

💡 Coordinate Communities

Websites like nmssaves.com and subreddits like r/NMSCoordinateExchange list exact portal coordinates for rare ship spawns, including specific Exotic designs, rare Living Ship variants, and S-class Haulers at known Trade Posts. Use these to skip hundreds of hours of searching.

💡 Dismantle for Nanites

When you upgrade a ship or tool module to a higher class, dismantle the old module — don't sell it. Dismantling gives you Nanite Clusters at a much better rate than selling, especially for A and S-class modules.

💡 Buying Ships from Freighters

Freighters have their own landing pads where NPC ships periodically dock. Hang out on a friendly freighter's hangar deck and check arriving ships the same way you would at a Space Station — sometimes you'll find a class or design you can't get elsewhere, including rare S-class haulers.

💡 Hauler Storage Stacking

Haulers have the highest base cargo (up to 120 slots at S-class) AND naturally high shield class bonuses. A fully upgraded S-class Hauler with 3 adjacent S-class shield modules is the most durable ship in the game while still carrying the most cargo — the best all-purpose endgame vessel for trading and survivability.

💡 Sentinel Interceptor Reroll

Before approaching an Interceptor wreck, save manually. Interact with the wreck to check its class. Reload if not S-class, and the wreck rerolls. The Hyaline Brain quest and Monolith trip only need to be done once per crash site — subsequent reloads keep the same Brain, so you can pop in and check stats fast.

💡 Colossus Mining Loop

For maximum bulk yield: summon Colossus → drive it onto an Activated Indium or other ore deposit → use the built-in Mining Laser to harvest at speed → its 40+ slot cargo holds an enormous stack → land your starship nearby, swap to it, and transfer the stack via Matter Beam straight to your freighter. Repeat. Fastest bulk gathering loop in the game.

Command Briefing

Freighters are capital ships that serve as your mobile base, storage hub, and fleet command center. Frigates are the smaller vessels that form your fleet, sent on automated expeditions for resources and rewards. Together they form the backbone of a late-game economy — with a maxed-out fleet, you can generate millions of units and tonnes of rare materials on autopilot.

Freighter Basics

A freighter is a massive starship capable of warping between star systems independently, carrying enormous cargo, housing your frigates, and functioning as a fully-buildable base. You can only own one freighter at a time, but can upgrade and customize it extensively.

Core Functions

🏠 Mobile Base

Build rooms, labs, storage, and facilities aboard your freighter — it travels with you across the galaxy.

📦 Massive Storage

Up to 48 inventory slots (upgradeable) for bulk resources. Store overflow materials you can't carry.

⚓ Fleet Hangar

Houses all your frigates. Launch expeditions, review fleet status, and accept returning missions here.

🔭 Command Deck

The bridge lets you plot hyperspace routes, warp the entire fleet, and manage fleet expeditions.

🌌 Jump Drive

Freighters can warp independently — no hyperdrive needed in your ship. Follow your freighter as a teleport anchor.

🛡 Fleet Combat

Freighters can engage in space battles with Sentinel Dreadnoughts, rewarding rare tech and nanites.

Acquiring a Freighter

The Free Freighter Method

You can obtain a freighter for free by completing the recurring Space Rescue mission. The mechanics:

1
Warp at least 5 systems — after roughly your fifth warp jump (and after 3 hours of real playtime) you'll arrive in a system with a freighter under pirate attack.
2
Defend the freighter — destroy all attacking pirate ships without hitting the freighter or its frigates with friendly fire.
3
Board and speak to the captain — land in the freighter's hangar and use the teleporter to reach the bridge.
4
Accept or decline — the captain offers the freighter for free. Crucially, if you decline, the next rescue is also free, and so on until you finally accept.
Reroll for a Better Ship

Freighters remain free until you accept one — there's no one-shot. So decline any C-class or B-class offer, wait 3 hours playtime + 5 warps, and a new rescue will trigger. The first one or two rescues will be smaller "system freighters" (max ~19 slots). From the third onward they're always larger Capital Freighters.

Buying a Freighter

Once you own a freighter, all subsequent ones must be purchased. Small C-class system freighters cost relatively little; large S-class Capital Freighters command very high prices. Factors:

  • Higher class (S > A > B > C) means better base stats and more technology slots
  • Capital Freighters have more inventory slots, longer hulls, and more interior build space than system freighters
  • Visual design (Sentinel, Venator-style, etc.) is largely cosmetic — class drives the stats
  • Save before warping into a new system to reroll the hull design and class of any rescue encounter
⚠
Note: Buying a new freighter automatically transfers your freighter base layout to the new ship — you don't have to rebuild from scratch. However, all installed technology modules stay with the old freighter and are lost, so plan upgrade purchases around the swap.

Freighter Base Building

Your freighter's interior can be built out using the same base-building system as planetary bases. You have dedicated room prefabs that only work aboard freighters, plus standard base parts.

Essential Freighter Rooms

🛰 Fleet Command Room

Houses the fleet management console. Required to send expeditions remotely once installed; the first room you'll want to build.

⚗ Refining Room

Place Medium and Large Refiners on the floor for large-scale resource processing aboard your freighter.

🌱 Hydroponics Bay

Grow crops aboard your ship. No planet needed — automate rare ingredient farming for crafting chains.

🔬 Scientific Research Station

Houses the Blueprint Analyser. Used to unlock blueprints and convert Salvaged Data into permanent upgrades.

🤖 Orbital Exocraft Materialiser

Lets you summon any Exocraft anywhere on any planet without needing a Geobay or Summoning Station first.

📡 Galactic Trade Terminal

Place a trade terminal aboard so you can sell goods from your freighter without going to a station.

Build Layout Tip

Freighter room prefabs connect via corridor pieces. Plan your layout before building — rooms must be accessible from the main corridor and cannot overlap with existing hull sections. The forward section offers the most open build space.

Specialist NPCs Aboard Your Freighter

You can hire specialist NPCs at your freighter base to unlock new technology, blueprints, and mission lines. Each specialist appears once their requirements are met and offers a series of progressive missions:

SpecialistMission Line
⚙ Overseer (Gek)Required first. Unlocks the construction tech tree and other specialists
🌾 Farmer (Gek)Unlocks crop blueprints, including everything needed for Stasis Devices
🔬 Scientist (Korvax)Unlocks advanced refining and processed product blueprints
🛡 Armourer (Vy'keen)Unlocks weapon upgrades and combat-related blueprints
👷 Exocraft TechnicianUnlocks all Exocraft Geobay blueprints and exocraft tech

The same specialist hierarchy applies to planetary bases — but having them aboard the freighter means they travel with you.

Frigates — Overview

Frigates are medium-sized starships that dock in your freighter's hangar. You recruit them from systems you visit and deploy them on automated expeditions. They return with resources, units, and rare rewards without you having to fly anywhere.

Recruiting Frigates

1
Find frigates in space — Frigates float in orbit in most systems, usually near a planet or the star.
2
Hail the frigate — Fly close and interact. Review its stats: class (S–C), type, and five stat ratings.
3
Purchase it — C-class frigates are inexpensive; S-class frigates command premium prices. Higher class = better stats and more trait slots.
4
It joins your fleet — The frigate flies to your freighter and docks. You can view it from the Fleet Management terminal.

Frigate Stats Explained

Each frigate has five stat categories rated from 1–10. These determine how well it performs on different expedition types:

StatRelevant Expedition Type
⚔ CombatAggressive, Sentinel, and Conflict expeditions
🔭 ExplorationSurvey, Discovery, and long-range expeditions
⚒ MiningResource extraction and industrial expeditions
⚙ EngineeringSalvage, repair, and construction missions
🌱 SupportTrade, resupply, and diplomatic missions
Stat Matching

Each expedition requires a certain combined stat total in the relevant category across all deployed frigates. Higher totals increase success chance and reward quality. Always match frigate types to the expedition type.

Fleet Fuel

Expeditions consume Frigate Fuel (crafted from Di-hydrogen only). The blueprint unlocks automatically after building the Fleet Command Room. Stock up before sending fleets:

  • 7× Di-hydrogen → 50 Tonnes of Frigate Fuel (the base crafting batch)
  • Most expeditions cost 1–4 fuel batches depending on distance and tier
  • With a large fleet, you'll need hundreds — mine Di-hydrogen deposits and farm it at a base
  • Frigate Fuel Efficiency upgrades in your freighter tech grid reduce consumption

Frigate Types & Stats

Each frigate is tagged with one primary type that indicates its role. Recruit a balanced fleet with multiple types to cover all expedition categories.

⚔ Combat

High combat stat. Send on conflict and pirate-hunting missions.

🔭 Explorer

High exploration stat. Essential for survey and long-range missions.

⚒ Industrial

High mining stat. Best for resource extraction missions.

⚙ Support

Balanced support stat. Trade and resupply expeditions.

S-Class Frigates

S-class frigates appear rarely in any system. When you spot one, always buy it regardless of type — their stat totals are far superior, and they have additional trait slots that provide unique bonuses to your fleet's performance.

Frigate Traits

Higher-class frigates have trait cards that provide permanent bonuses. These are fixed when you purchase the frigate. Examples include:

  • Veteran Crew — +2 to all stats permanently
  • Lucky — Increased chance of bonus reward on expeditions
  • Fast Engine — Reduces expedition duration by 15–25%
  • Scavenger — Additional salvaged materials returned per expedition
  • Disciplined — Reduced damage taken during conflict expeditions

Fleet Expeditions

Expeditions are automated missions sent from your Fleet Command Room terminal. Select the expedition, assign frigates, and wait real-time hours for them to return. They are the primary passive income engine of the game.

Sending an Expedition

1
Open Fleet Operations — Accessible from the terminal in your freighter's hangar bay or bridge.
2
Select an expedition — Each expedition shows type, required stat total, duration, fuel cost, and potential rewards.
3
Assign frigates — The system shows a success percentage. Aim for 90%+ to minimize damage and failures.
4
Confirm and launch — Frigates depart from the hangar. Duration is real-time (1–20+ hours depending on tier).
5
Collect rewards — When frigates return, access the Fleet Command Room terminal to claim the haul and repair any damage.

Expedition Tiers

TierDurationFuel CostReward Scale
Tier 11–3 hrs1 fuelBasic resources, small units
Tier 23–8 hrs2 fuelRare resources, moderate units
Tier 38–16 hrs3–4 fuelHigh-value materials, large units
Tier 416–24+ hrs4–6 fuelUnique items, massive rewards

Expedition Outcomes

✅ Success Full
⚠ Partial ~60%
💥 Failure Loss

Partial success = reduced rewards + frigate damage. Failure = no rewards + heavy damage (frigates can be destroyed at very low success %). Always target 90%+ success.

Repairing Frigates

Damaged frigates are flagged red in your fleet view. To repair them: land on the frigate, follow the audio cue to the damaged component, and interact with it to supply the required resources (typically combinations of Metal Plating, Ferrite Dust, Chromatic Metal, Carbon Nanotubes, and similar materials). Each damaged subsystem has its own resource requirement.

  • Check frigate health before every expedition — damaged frigates perform worse and may be destroyed
  • Never send frigates with <30% health on long expeditions
  • Keep common repair materials in your freighter inventory — Metal Plating and Carbon Nanotubes are used most often
  • Generic Repair Kits (found in Black Market Terminals and Nexus missions) can substitute for any single missing component

Pro Tips & Strategy

💡 Capital Ship Battles

Sentinel Dreadnoughts occasionally appear in systems. Defeating them rewards Salvaged Frigate Modules — the currency used to purchase the best freighter technology upgrades. Fight one every time you encounter one.

💡 Save Scum Freighters

Before warping into a system that might have a rescue event, manual save. If the freighter class or design is poor, reload and warp again. Repeat until you find an S-class design you want.

💡 Squadron Pilots

You can hire NPC pilots to your squadron from any Space Station — they fly alongside you in dogfights. Up to four squad mates; recruiting them requires Nanites. They use your collection of owned ships, so build a stable of fighters you don't mind handing out.

⚡
Fuel Warning: If you send 5+ frigates on Tier 3–4 expeditions simultaneously, you'll burn through Frigate Fuel fast. Always keep a large stack of Di-hydrogen in freighter storage so you can craft more without scrambling.
💡 Matter Beam is Crucial

Install the Matter Beam upgrade as early as possible. Being able to dump resources directly into your freighter's inventory while on a planet — without flying back — completely transforms resource gathering efficiency.

💡 Teleport to Your Freighter

Your freighter appears as a teleport destination from any Space Station terminal. Use this to instantly warp back to collect expedition rewards without burning hyperdrive fuel. Combine with the freighter warping to new systems to build an efficient loop.

💡 Parallel Expeditions

You can run multiple expeditions in parallel, one per active mission. Each expedition uses 1–5 frigates depending on its stat requirements. Once you have 8–10 frigates, you can typically have 2–3 missions running at once. Sending more frigates than required to a mission boosts success chance.

💡 Freighter Farming Loop

End-game loop: Land on freighter → collect expedition rewards → launch new expeditions → transfer resources via Matter Beam while planet farming → teleport back. A well-managed fleet generates 5–20M units per real-time day entirely passively.

Recommended Frigate Fleet Progression

1
2–3 frigates (any type) — Get started with Tier 1–2 expeditions. Don't worry about class yet; learn the system.
2
5 frigates (balanced types) — One of each: Combat, Explorer, Industrial, Support, and one wildcard. Run Tier 2 expeditions profitably.
3
8 frigates (A-class minimum) — Start replacing C/B-class with A and S. Match frigate types to expedition types for higher success. Run Tier 3 expeditions.
4
10–12 frigates (S-class majority) — Endgame fleet. 90%+ success on Tier 4 expeditions. Split into multiple parallel expedition groups.

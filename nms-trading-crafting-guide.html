The Economy System

No Man's Sky uses a dynamic, galaxy-wide economy where every star system has an economy type that determines what it buys at premium and what it stocks cheaply. Learning this system is the foundation of profitable trading.

Economy Types

⚒ Mining Sells: Ores, metals Buys: Tech, refined goods
🌱 Agricultural Sells: Organic compounds Buys: Nutrients, gas
⚗ Chemical Sells: Gases, chemicals Buys: Minerals, alloys
🔬 Scientific Sells: Research materials Buys: Raw resources
⚙ Manufacturing Sells: Processed goods Buys: Components
💱 Trading Sells: General goods Buys: Everything at ↑price

Economy Strength

Each system also has a wealth tier — Struggling, Moderate, Comfortable, Wealthy, or Opulent. Higher-wealth systems:

  • Have more Trade Terminal stock at once
  • Pay better prices for commodities
  • Stock more advanced technology blueprints
  • Have more NPCs willing to trade starships at reduced cost
💡 Best Systems

Target Opulent Trading systems for selling. They pay premium prices across the board. Look for the gold star icon in the Galactic Map when filtering by economy.

Items are priced relative to the galactic average. Green price = above average (sell here). Red price = below average (buy here). The system fluctuates, but economy types reliably favor certain goods.

What to Sell & What to Keep

This is the most practical question in the game. The wrong answer costs you either units or critical crafting resources. Use this ledger as your reference.

Raw Resources

⚠ Rule of Thumb

If an item is used as a crafting ingredient for something that sells for 10× its value — keep it and craft. If it has no profitable crafting path, sell immediately. Trade commodities (items found randomly in crates/missions) are always sell — they have zero crafting use.

Trading Mechanics

Where to Trade

🛸 Space Station

Main Trade Terminal. Always accessible. Good baseline prices for common goods.

🪐 Planetary Trade Post

Smaller terminals on planet surfaces. Often hidden from main markets — can have unique commodities.

🚀 NPC Traders

Ships landing at Trade Posts and Stations. Buy/sell directly with them for varied prices.

🌌 Freighter Market

Capital ships have their own Trade Terminal. Can have different stock and prices than the station.

The Trade Route Method

1
Identify a surplus item in a Mining or Agricultural system — it shows green (cheap price).
2
Buy the maximum stack — fill your cargo hold (up to 500 units per stack).
3
Fly to the adjacent system that needs that item (opposite economy type). Check the Galactic Map for nearby systems.
4
Sell at the Trade Terminal — price should now be red (they want it). Profit the difference.
5
Buy their surplus for the return trip and repeat.
Price Manipulation

Selling large quantities in one terminal tanks the local price. To maximize returns, split your stack across multiple terminals in the same system (Space Station + Trade Post + Freighter terminal), or sell across multiple visits on different days.

Hauler Ship Advantage

Hauler-class ships have bonus cargo slots and increased stack limits. For serious trading, a high-class Hauler with maxed storage is far superior to any other ship class — more cargo per trip = more profit per run.

⚠ Note on Activated Indium

Activated Indium's sale value was significantly nerfed in update 4.0 (Waypoint) — it now sells for much less than it once did. Don't sell it raw. Refine into Chromatic Metal (1:4 ratio, or 1:8 with Pure Ferrite) for substantially better returns.

Stasis Device vs Fusion Ignitor

Both sell for extremely high value — among the best single-item payouts in the game. The Stasis Device chain uses more biological/organic farming (Frost Crystal, Solanium, Star Bulb, Gamma Root); the Fusion Ignitor chain leans on Fungal Mould plus minerals/tech. Set up both production lines eventually — they share few inputs.

Crafting Basics

Crafting transforms cheap raw materials into high-value goods. The deeper the crafting chain (more steps), the higher the value multiplier. Simple refining can triple value; full crafting chains like the Stasis Device yield 50–100× value on raw inputs.

Crafting Stations

🎒 Personal Inventory

Basic crafting from your exosuit. Recipes already known by default. Used anywhere, anytime.

🔩 Basic Refiner

1-input, 1-output refining. Portable, placeable. Essential early-game tool.

⚗ Medium Refiner

2-input refining. Unlocks more complex and valuable recipes. Build at base.

🏭 Large Refiner

3-input refining for the most complex and lucrative recipes. Must be in a base.

🌿 Portable Refiner

Deployable anywhere on planets. Tiny capacity — used for emergency refining in the field.

🧪 Nutrient Processor

Turns crops into cooked food with nanite value. Underused but profitable with the right farm.

Learning Recipes

  • Default recipes are available from the start in your inventory menu
  • Blueprint Analyser at your base reveals new crafting schematics for Units
  • Iteration: Hyperion (Synthesis Laboratory) on the Space Anomaly sells crafting blueprints for nanites
  • Completing story missions and talking to aliens unlocks unique recipes
  • Abandoned buildings and derelict freighters contain rare blueprints
⚠ Missing a Recipe?

If you can't craft something you've seen listed, you likely haven't learned the blueprint yet. Visit the Synthesis Laboratory (Iteration: Hyperion) on the Space Anomaly and browse the full blueprint catalogue.

💡 Chlorine Doubling Loop

Put 1 Chlorine + 2 Oxygen in a Medium Refiner to get 6 Chlorine. Or start from scratch with 2 Salt + 2 Oxygen → 5 Chlorine. This is the fastest unit-per-hour loop in early-to-mid game. Buy Chlorine and Oxygen at every Space Station, then loop indefinitely.

Earning Nanite Clusters

Nanites are the secondary currency used for technology upgrades, S-class module purchases, and blueprint unlocks. They cannot be crafted directly — you must farm them through specific activities.

Best Nanite Sources

MethodNanites/HourNotes
Scrapping Starships Very High Buy ships from station hangars, scrap at the Scrap Dealer for modules. Top method overall.
Runaway Mould Refining Very High Mould refines 1:1 to Nanites. Find Curious Deposits of Mould (top passive method when near a large deposit).
Salvaged Data Refining Steady Salvaged Data refines 1:15 into Nanites. Dig up Buried Technology with the Terrain Manipulator.
Sentinel Combat (Salvaged Glass) High Killing Sentinel Drones drops Salvaged Glass, which contains procedural upgrade modules to sell.
Analyse Discoveries Medium Scan undiscovered flora, fauna, and minerals. Good per-planet yield — scan everything on undiscovered worlds for best returns.
Space Station Missions Medium Some missions reward nanites directly. Refresh missions every few minutes by leaving and returning.
Cook Food (Nutrient Processor) Medium Many cooked food items sell for nanites at the cooking NPCs and stations. Requires crop farming.
Derelict Freighters High (per run) Loot Salvaged Frigate Modules, modules, and Hadal Cores (refine 1:50 to Nanites). Solid yield per run with varied loot.
Tainted Metal Refining Medium Low individual yield but accumulates fast in active combat. Drops from corrupted Sentinels.
Frigate Expeditions Passive Some expedition rewards include nanite caches.

The Mould Farm (Best Passive Method)

1
Find a Curious Deposit of Runaway Mould — A renewable rocky deposit on certain planets that respawns. Look for the orange/brown Mould texture, or use Anomaly Detectors.
2
Set up a base nearby — Place a base computer and a row of refiners adjacent to the deposit so you can run Mould directly into Nanites without traveling.
3
Refine in any refiner — Runaway Mould → Nanite Clusters at 1:1, no additional inputs needed.
4
Stack refiners — Multiple Basic Refiners running in parallel let you process Mould as fast as you can harvest. Very high nanite output is achievable from a single deposit when stacking refiners.
💡 Spend Nanites On

Priority order: (1) S-class upgrade modules from Tech Merchants and the Anomaly for your ship/multi-tool/exosuit. (2) Freighter technology modules. (3) Blueprints from the Synthesis Laboratory on the Anomaly. Never spend nanites on C-class modules — always wait for S-class.

Farming & Trade Loops

The richest players use automated farming loops — setups that produce resources or money with minimal active input. Here are the best loops at each stage.

Early Game Loop — Chlorine Expansion

Requires: Medium Refiner + Oxygen Supply

Buy Chlorine and Oxygen at Space Stations → combine 1 Chlorine + 2 Oxygen → 6 Chlorine in a Medium Refiner → sell most, recycle some → repeat. Build a base Oxygen Harvester or set up Gas Extractors on an Oxygen hotspot to scale. Excellent unit income at full scale.

Mid Game Loop — Activated Indium Farm

1
Find a Blue Star system — Activated Indium only spawns on planets orbiting blue stars (requires the Indium Drive on your ship).
2
Build a base on the deposit — Place Mineral Extractors on the Activated Indium hotspot. Connect to Supply Depots and power via a Solar Panel or Electromagnetic Generator.
3
Refine for value — Raw sell value was significantly reduced in update 4.0 (Waypoint). Refine into Chromatic Metal (1:4, or 1:8 with Pure Ferrite) for far better value, or use the raw stack as inputs for advanced refining chains.
4
Collect passively — Mineral Extractors produce continuously while you're elsewhere. Visit periodically to empty the Supply Depots.

Late Game Loop — Stasis Device Chain

1
Build a large crop farm — Biodomes with Frost Crystal, Solanium, Star Bulb, Cactus Flesh, Gamma Root, Fungal Mould, plus Gas Extractors for Radon, Nitrogen, and Sulphurine.
2
Set up multiple Large Refiners — Crops refine into Enriched Carbon, Nitrogen Salt, Thermic Condensate, Semiconductor, Superconductor, Heat Capacitor, Poly Fiber, and Circuit Board in cascading chains.
3
Craft the final products — Quantum Processor + Cryogenic Chamber + Iridesite = Stasis Device (~15.6M). Or Portable Reactor + Quantum Processor + Geodesite = Fusion Ignitor (~15.6M).
4
Sell batch at the Galactic Trade Terminal — Each batch sold yields extremely high unit income. Cycle between systems to avoid saturating one market.

Pro Tips & Secrets

💡 Stack Size Matters

In Normal mode, Exosuit Cargo slots hold up to 9,999 of any basic resource per slot. Trade goods, glass, and curiosities cap lower (50 per slot), but raw mats and refined items stack high. Max out cargo upgrades on your Exosuit (up to 120 slots) and your Hauler ship (up to 120 slots) for serious trading.

💡 Exocraft for Bulk Mining

For large-scale Activated Indium or other ore farming, use the Colossus Exocraft with its huge cargo hold (42 slots) and a Mining Laser upgrade. Far faster than Multi-Tool mining, and its inventory doesn't fill your suit — transfer from Colossus straight to a base storage container.

💡 Market Saturation

Each terminal has a limited buy order — after you sell a large batch, its available supply/demand resets. Wait 2–3 in-game days (or visit a different terminal) after bulk selling to get back to peak prices for the same commodity.

💡 Derelict Freighters = Hidden Wealth

Buy Emergency Broadcast Receivers at Space Stations to locate Derelict Freighters. Inside: Salvaged Frigate Modules (freighter tech currency), valuable cargo crates, Hadal Cores, and rare blueprints. One full run takes 10–20 minutes and yields several million units plus thousands of nanites in mixed loot.

💡 Ship Scrapping for Nanites

The Scrap Dealer on Space Stations dismantles starships in exchange for Upgrade Modules and Storage Augmentation Modules. Cheap ships traded for the Scrap Dealer yield modules that sell to Technology Merchants for nanites — one of the highest nanite-per-hour methods in the game.

💡 Use Your Freighter Storage

Before you have the Matter Beam, make a habit of landing on your freighter to offload bulk goods between every few planets. Running full prevents you from picking up better items. The freighter is free storage — use it aggressively.

💡 Galactic Map Filter

In the Galactic Map, filter by Economy Type and Wealth. Star systems show their economy in the sidebar. Build a trade route between two adjacent systems of opposite economy type (e.g., Mining next to Manufacturing) for consistent buy-low/sell-high runs.

💡 Don't Sell Upgrade Modules

When you dismantle old technology from a ship or suit, you get Upgrade Modules back. Never sell these — they can be reinstalled or traded to a Technology Merchant on Space Stations for Nanites at a much better rate than selling to the Trade Terminal.

💡 Fastest Early Money

Just arrived in a new system? Check the Space Station Trade Terminal immediately for items with a green price tag. These are in-demand commodities at above-average prices. Even selling 200 units of a common resource at 30% above average is fast early profit requiring zero farming.

💡 Race to the Large Refiner

The single biggest crafting unlock is the Large Refiner. Build one at your base as soon as possible — it unlocks the 3-input recipe tier, which includes all the most profitable processing chains in the game. Prioritize getting this before any other base expansion.

Multi-Tools

The Multi-Tool is your handheld device for mining, scanning, and combat. Like ships, they come in multiple types and classes with significant stat differences.

Multi-Tool Types

🔫 Pistol Balanced

Compact and portable. Good mining and decent combat. Best starter type. Widely available.

🪖 Rifle Combat Focus

High damage output. More tech slots than pistol. Best for sentinel fighting builds.

⚗ Experimental Scanner Specialist

Highest scan bonuses. Best for discovering flora and fauna for nanite income.

🌿 Alien Mining Specialist

Best mining bonuses. Top choice for efficient resource gathering. Harder to find.

🔮 Royal Premium

Rare, beautiful designs with excellent balanced stats. Found in specific systems by following bird-like creatures to Royal cabinets.

🤖 Sentinel Combat / Damage

Recovered from cabinets at Harmonic Camps on Dissonant planets after solving the override-glyph puzzle. High damage bonus, free but starts with damaged slots.

💎 Atlantid Crystalline Rare

Crystalline multi-tool style. Found via specific quest interactions and at certain settlements. Distinct glowing aesthetic.

Finding Multi-Tools

  • Space Station cabinets — the display case in the room past the Exosuit upgrade NPC. One tool per station.
  • Alien NPCs at stations and Trade Posts sometimes offer their personal tool for purchase
  • Minor Settlements have a Multi-Tool cabinet in the Trade Terminal building — often with rarer types
  • Harmonic Camps on Dissonant planets contain Sentinel Multi-Tools after solving the Multi-Tool seal puzzle
  • Space Anomaly — the Multi-Tool Decommissioning Terminal next to Iteration: Tethys lets you scrap old tools for nanites
  • Save-scumming works for tools too: reload before interacting with a cabinet to reroll class and stats

Key Multi-Tool Upgrades

⛏ Mining Beam Resource Gathering

Upgrade to increase mining speed and heat tolerance. Stack 3 for near-instant resource extraction.

💥 Boltcaster Combat

Primary combat weapon. Stack 3 upgrades for serious damage output against sentinels.

🌊 Scatter Blaster Close Combat

Shotgun-style close-range weapon. Devastating against single targets at short range.

🔭 Analysis Visor Scanner

Upgrades reveal more resource deposits and increase scan bonus per discovery. Key for nanite farming.

💣 Plasma Launcher Area Damage

Fires explosive rounds. Excellent for clearing groups of sentinels. Also useful for terrain manipulation.

🔬 Terrain Manipulator Excavation

Digs and places terrain. Essential for cave mining and base building. Upgrade for faster excavation.

Pro Tips & Secrets

💡 Own Multiple Ships

You can own up to 12 ships simultaneously. Keep a dedicated Fighter for combat, an Explorer for long warps, a Hauler for trading runs, and your favorite for daily use. Swap between them via the Quick Menu anywhere.

💡 Exotic Ships — Always S-Class

Exotic ships are always S-class with no exceptions, and always have a unique visual design that appears nowhere else. They're smaller than standard ships but their stats punch above their size. If you see an Exotic land, buy it immediately — it may never return.

💡 Coordinate Communities

Websites like nmssaves.com and subreddits like r/NMSCoordinateExchange list exact portal coordinates for rare ship spawns, including specific Exotic designs, rare Living Ship variants, and S-class Haulers at known Trade Posts. Use these to skip hundreds of hours of searching.

💡 Dismantle for Nanites

When you upgrade a ship or tool module to a higher class, dismantle the old module — don't sell it. Dismantling gives you Nanite Clusters at a much better rate than selling, especially for A and S-class modules.

💡 Buying Ships from Freighters

Freighters have their own landing pads where NPC ships periodically dock. Hang out on a friendly freighter's hangar deck and check arriving ships the same way you would at a Space Station — sometimes you'll find a class or design you can't get elsewhere, including rare S-class haulers.

💡 Sentinel Interceptor Reroll

Before approaching an Interceptor wreck, save manually. Interact with the wreck to check its class. Reload if not S-class, and the wreck rerolls. The Hyaline Brain quest and Monolith trip only need to be done once per crash site — subsequent reloads keep the same Brain, so you can pop in and check stats fast.

💡 Colossus Mining Loop

For maximum bulk yield: summon Colossus → drive it onto an Activated Indium or other ore deposit → use the built-in Mining Laser to harvest at speed → its 40+ slot cargo holds an enormous stack → land your starship nearby, swap to it, and transfer the stack via Matter Beam straight to your freighter. Repeat. Fastest bulk gathering loop in the game.

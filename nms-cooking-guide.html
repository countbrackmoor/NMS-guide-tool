The Nutrient Processor

Cooking in No Man's Sky is done entirely through the Nutrient Processor — a portable base building device that combines raw and processed ingredients into edible consumables. It's not a recipe memorisation game; it's a combinatorial discovery system. Experiment freely.

Key Mechanics

  • 3 input slots — combine one, two, or three ingredients at once. The output updates live as you add ingredients, so you can preview recipes before committing
  • No fuel required — unlike Refiners, the Nutrient Processor runs without Carbon or power input
  • 2.5 seconds per batch — all recipes take the same time regardless of complexity
  • 1:1 ratio — one unit of each ingredient in produces one unit of output. To produce 10 dishes, you need 10 of each ingredient
  • Ingredient Storage — 50 shared slots (up to 50 units each) accessible across all Nutrient Processors you own. Keeps your main inventory free
Getting the Blueprint

Purchase the Nutrient Processor blueprint at the Construction Research Station aboard the Space Anomaly for 10 Salvaged Data. It's one of the most underrated early unlocks — buy it as soon as you have the data.

Catalogue Trick

You don't have to actually cook a dish to record it in your catalogue. Arrange the right ingredients in the Processor so the output appears in the output slot — then simply don't press confirm. The recipe gets catalogued silently. Useful for completionists who want to log dishes without wasting ingredients.

Using Multiple Processors

Place two or three Nutrient Processors side-by-side and run parallel batches. Since each stage of a multi-step recipe takes 2.5 seconds, a triple-processor setup lets you process intermediates in two machines while the third combines them into the final dish — roughly tripling your throughput.

Ingredient Sourcing

Ingredients come from four primary sources. Most serious cooking setups automate at least the flora side via farms, supplemented by creature harvesting and trade post purchases.

🌿 Flora Harvestable Plants

Scan plants with your Multi-Tool Analysis Visor to identify food-producing species. Key plants: Fungal Mould (batter base), Frost Crystal / Frostwort (pastry base), Solanium (sweeteners), Gamma Weed (stew base), Cactus Flesh (extracts).

🐾 Fauna Creature Products

Tame creatures with Creature Pellets to harvest Milk, Eggs, Honey, and other byproducts. Kill creatures for Mordite (meat substitute) and organ drops. Use Livestock Units + Automated Feeders at your base for passive production.

🐟 Fish Aquarius Update+

Caught with a Fishing Rig placed in water. Fish are ingredients in a range of seafood recipes added in the Aquarius update. Bait (including cooked bait) increases catch size and rarity. A new and growing recipe category.

🛒 Trade Posts Purchased

NPC pilots at Trade Posts and Space Stations often sell raw food ingredients like Sweet Root, Pulpy Roots, and Grahj'am. Check regularly — stock resets on station visits. Useful for rare ingredients you can't farm.

🌾 Farming Base / Freighter

The most efficient source. Build Biodomes or Hydroponic Trays and plant food crops for a passive, infinite supply. A freighter farm can provide enough raw material for hundreds of dishes per day without any planet-side effort.

✨ Processed Nutrient Processor

Many ingredients only exist as outputs of the Processor itself — batters, custards, creams, and jams can only be made by cooking, not found in the wild. These intermediate products are the backbone of complex recipes.

Farm Priority List

If you're building a serious cooking operation, grow these first — they unlock the widest range of recipes:

  • Fungal Mould → processes into the batter and dough family, the core of most baked goods
  • Frost Crystal / Frostwort → produces glass grains and pastry components
  • Solanium → sugar and sweetener base for higher-tier desserts
  • Gamma Weed → stew and soup base
  • Cactus Flesh → cactus nectar, used in a wide variety of recipes (single-process from raw)
  • Any docile fauna for milk and eggs via Livestock Unit + Automated Feeder

Processing Chains

The core insight of NMS cooking: raw ingredients rarely make the best dishes directly. Process raw materials into intermediate products first, then combine intermediates to reach high-value final dishes. Think of it like real cooking — you turn wheat into flour before making bread.

The Rule

Always ask: "Can I run this raw ingredient through the Processor first?" Almost every raw ingredient has a single-ingredient processing step that produces a more useful intermediate. Selling or eating the raw form is almost always worse than taking the extra step.

Example Chains — Baking

Batter Chain
Fungal Mould → Proto-Batter + Eggs / Milk → Cake Batter + Cream → Cream Buns (50% Hazard)
Proto-Batter is a single-ingredient step from Fungal Mould. Each step = 2.5 seconds in the Processor.
Custard Chain
Milk / Fauna Byproduct → Viscous Custard + Cake Batter → Custard Fancy (50% Hazard)
High-Value Batter Upgrade Chain
Cake Batter + ingredient → Extra-Fluffy Batter → Wailing Batter → Writhing, Roiling Batter
Higher-tier batters produce Jetpack Boost dishes. Each step up increases the output value and Cronus nanite reward.
Syrupy Batter Chain (Health Restoration)
Syrupy Nectar or Butter Syrup → Syrupy Batter + Cream / Custard / etc. → Syrupy dishes (50% Health)
Syrupy Batter is rarer to produce but yields 50% health restoration — the most powerful consumable effect in the game.
Batter Tier Recap

Batter complexity determines the output buff tier. Cake Batter → Hazard Protection. Thick, Sweet Batter → Stamina Boost. Extra-Fluffy / Wailing / Writhing Batter → Jetpack Boost. Syrupy Batter → Health Restoration. Upgrading your batter one step before cooking is always worth it.

Cronus & Nanite Farming

Iteration: Cronus is an NPC on the Space Anomaly who operates a grill and will buy your cooked food for Nanite Clusters. He pays significantly more than standard terminals for high-tier dishes, making cooking one of the most efficient passive Nanite income streams in the game.

Cronus's Grill

  • Found on the Space Anomaly near the back — look for the grill station with the alien NPC
  • Bring a stack of cooked dishes and sell them all at once. Higher-tier dishes (complex multi-step recipes) earn substantially more nanites per item
  • Nanite payouts have some randomness — the same dish won't always pay the same amount, but more complex dishes have a consistently higher payout range
  • There's no cooldown or sell limit — sell as many as you can make
Best Dishes for Cronus

Focus on dishes using Writhing, Roiling Batter or Wailing Batter as the base — these are the highest-tier batters and produce Jetpack Boost dishes that Cronus values most. A well-optimised cooking farm producing these high-tier bakes can generate thousands of Nanites per Anomaly visit.

Setting Up a Nanite Cooking Farm

1
Farm Fungal Mould — plant it in multiple Biodomes. This is the root of every batter chain. The bigger the farm, the more dishes per run.
2
Harvest creature byproducts — set up a Livestock Unit and Automated Feeder with docile fauna to passively generate Milk, Cream, and Eggs for custard and cream production.
3
Set up 3 Nutrient Processors side-by-side. Run Fungal Mould through the first to produce Proto-Batter; combine Proto-Batter with creature products for Cake Batter in the second; upgrade Cake Batter to higher-tier batter in the third.
4
Combine high-tier batter with Cream, Jams, or Custard to produce the final dish. Use all three processors in rotation to run parallel batches.
5
Visit Cronus on the Anomaly and sell everything. Restock from Ingredient Storage (which refills as your farm runs), and repeat.
Catalogue Completionism & Nanites

Every unique dish you cook for the first time earns a small Nanite bonus on top of the Cronus sale. Working through all 250+ recipes systematically using the catalogue trick (see section 01) nets a surprisingly large one-time windfall — worth doing early if you're chasing Nanites.

Pro Tips

💡 Only one buff at a time

Eating a new food item replaces any active buff — it doesn't stack. Choose your meal based on what you're about to do: Jetpack boost for platforming, Hazard Protection for hostile environments, Stamina for long sprints, Health for combat.

💡 Dedicate a Storage Container

Raw cooking ingredients and processed intermediates fill up fast. Dedicate Storage Container 9 (or any unused number) to cooking supplies — one slot type per ingredient. Label it with a Message Module. Your Ingredient Storage (in the Processor itself) handles 50 slots of active pantry; the container is your long-term stockpile.

💡 Systematic discovery

To discover new recipes efficiently, take one processed ingredient (like Cake Batter) and try it with every other single ingredient you have, one at a time. You'll fill your catalogue far faster than experimenting randomly — and you'll spot the pattern of what each base ingredient produces across different batter tiers.

💡 Never sell raw ingredients

Almost every raw ingredient is worth more processed than sold directly. Even a single Nutrient Processor step multiplies the value. Two or three steps multiplies it further. The Cronus nanite value of a complex dish can be 10–20× the raw sell value of its constituent ingredients.

💡 Freighter farm is the best kitchen

A freighter base with Hydroponic Trays for cooking crops means your pantry is always stocked regardless of which planet you're on. Set up crop automation on the freighter first, then worry about planetary bases for scale later.

💡 Share food on the Anomaly

NMS has a lovely community tradition of players distributing home-cooked food to strangers on the Anomaly. Drop a stack of high-tier dishes near the entrance before visiting Cronus — it costs you a few seconds and makes someone's session.

Base Fundamentals

Every base starts with a Base Computer. It defines your claim on the land, lets you upload the base to servers (making it visible to other players), rename it, and delete it. You can have multiple bases across different planets and systems.

Placing a Base Computer

  • Craft the Base Computer from the build menu (no research required — it's free from the start)
  • Place it on any planetary surface. This becomes the centre of your base radius
  • The default base radius is 300u (units). Every part you build inside this sphere "belongs" to the base
  • You can extend the radius up to roughly 1,000u by building structures at the edge — the game expands the boundary to include them
Choosing a Site

Before placing the Base Computer, scout the area. Ideal base locations have: a nearby resource hotspot (electromagnetic, mineral, or gas), low or zero sentinel activity, minimal environmental hazards, and — if you care about aesthetics — a good view. Use the Survey Device on your Multi-Tool or Exocraft to scan for hotspots before committing.

Blueprints & Salvaged Data

Most base parts must be researched before you can build them. Visit the Construction Research Station aboard the Space Anomaly and spend Salvaged Data (found by digging up Buried Technology Modules on any planet) to unlock blueprints. Blueprints unlock permanently across all saves.

Base Salvaging & Dismantling

  • Dismantling individual parts returns ~50% of resources used to build them
  • Deleting a base via the Base Computer returns resources to a "Base Salvage Capsule" that appears near where the computer was
  • You can also delete bases remotely from any other Base Computer's management screen

Structure Types

NMS offers multiple building systems that can be mixed and matched. Each has a distinct aesthetic and different snap-point behaviour.

🏗 Prefab Rooms Cuboid / Round / Large

Pre-made enclosed rooms that snap together via corridors. Cuboid rooms are the most versatile. Includes glass variants, viewing domes, and large round rooms for multi-purpose hubs.

🪵 Wooden Parts Basic Modular

Foundations, walls, floors, roofs, stairs. Cheapest tier. Snap together in a modular grid. Great for large open structures and starting bases. Requires Carbon.

🔩 Metal Parts Industrial Modular

Same grid system as wood but with a rugged industrial look. Requires Ferrite Dust. Sturdier aesthetic — good for outposts and hangars.

✨ Alloy Parts Advanced Modular

Sleek, futuristic panels. Require Pure Ferrite. The cleanest aesthetic tier — ideal for polished endgame builds and landing pads.

🔲 Glass & Panes Transparent Parts

Glass walls, floors, and cuboid frames. Require Glass (Silicate Powder × 40 → Glass in a Refiner). Essential for greenhouses, observation decks, and underwater visibility.

🎨 Decoration Furniture / Lights / Plants

Posters, shelves, planters, terminals, light panels, flags. Purely cosmetic but make the difference between a base and a home. Many unlocked at the Quicksilver Synthesis Companion.

⚠ Terrain Regeneration

If you dig out terrain with the Terrain Manipulator and build inside it, the terrain will regenerate over time and clip through your base. Build above ground or on flattened surfaces. Underground bases are possible but require careful planning — prefab rooms resist terrain regrowth better than modular parts.

Snap Points & Free Placement

All structural parts use a snap-point grid. Hold the build menu button to cycle between snap positions when placing parts. For decorative items and furniture, toggle Free Placement mode (shown in the build menu) to position things without the grid — essential for custom layouts.

Power Systems

Most functional base components — lights, doors, teleporters, extractors — require power. Power is measured in kP (kiloPower). Open the build menu wiring view to see your base's power supply vs. demand in real time.

Power Sources

🔥 Biofuel Reactor ~50 kP · Fuel-Based

Burns Carbon or Condensed Carbon for power. Finite fuel — must be manually refilled. Earliest available generator. Good starter source, but doesn't scale well. One reactor supports basic lighting and a teleporter.

☀ Solar Panel ~50 kP · Daylight Only

Generates ~50 kP during daytime, zero at night. Pair with Batteries for 24/7 power. The most popular mid-game power solution. No fuel, no hotspot — works everywhere.

⚡ Electromagnetic Generator ~50–300 kP · Infinite

Free infinite power — but requires placement on an Electromagnetic Hotspot. Output depends on hotspot class: C (~75), B (~150), A (~225), S (~300). The best endgame power source. Stack multiple generators on one S-class hotspot for massive output.

🔋 Battery ~45,000 kP Storage

Stores excess power for later. Essential companion to Solar Panels for nighttime. Each Battery stores ~45,000 kP. Rule of thumb: 2 Solar Panels per 1 Battery for reliable 24/7 power.

Finding Electromagnetic Hotspots

Equip the Survey Device on your Multi-Tool (or Exocraft) and activate it on a planet's surface. It shows nearby hotspot markers with class ratings. Electromagnetic hotspots appear as a lightning bolt icon. Walk toward the strongest signal to find the centre — place your generators there for maximum output. S-class EM hotspots are rare but worth seeking out; a single one can power an entire mega-base.

Wiring Power

Use Electrical Wiring to connect power sources to consumers. Enter wiring mode in the build menu, click a power source's output node, then click the consumer's input node. Wires can branch — one source can feed multiple devices. Wire length is limited but generous; for long runs, chain through Electrical Cloaking Units to hide wires aesthetically.

Power Budget Tip

The build menu's wiring view shows total supply and total demand for your entire base. If demand exceeds supply, components furthest from the power source may flicker or stop working. Always over-provision by ~20% to account for future expansion.

Resource Extraction

Autonomous resource gathering is the backbone of an industrial base. Place extractors on hotspots and let them fill Supply Depots while you explore.

Extractors & Harvesters

⛏ Mineral Extractor Solid Resources

Place on a Mineral Hotspot to passively extract whatever mineral that hotspot contains (Magnetised Ferrite, Pure Ferrite, Pyrite, Paraffinium, etc.). Output depends on hotspot class. Requires power. Connect to Supply Depots for storage.

💨 Gas Extractor Gaseous Resources

Place on a Gas Hotspot to extract Sulphurine, Radon, or Nitrogen (depends on planet biome). Same mechanics as Mineral Extractor. Gas resources are key ingredients for high-value crafting recipes.

🌬 Atmosphere Harvester Passive Gas Collection

Does NOT require a hotspot — place anywhere on a planet. Passively collects small amounts of atmospheric gases (Oxygen, Nitrogen, Sulphurine, or Radon depending on biome). Slower than Gas Extractors but zero setup cost. Requires power.

📦 Supply Depot Storage Silo

Large storage containers that connect to extractors. One extractor fills one depot; chain multiple depots to a single extractor for massive stockpiles. Each depot holds ~1,000 units. Check and collect from the depot's terminal.

Setting Up an Autonomous Mining Base

1
Survey — use the Survey Device to find a mineral or gas hotspot. Note its class (S/A/B/C). Higher class = higher extraction rate.
2
Place Base Computer — drop it within range of the hotspot. If there's also an EM hotspot nearby, place the computer between them.
3
Build extractors on the hotspot centre. Stack multiple extractors for higher output (diminishing returns after ~4–5).
4
Wire to Supply Depots — connect extractors to depots with pipes (placed automatically when connecting output to depot input).
5
Power everything — wire an EM Generator (ideal) or Solar Panel + Battery array to the extractors. Extractors without power do nothing.
6
Leave and return — resources accumulate even when you're off-planet. Visit periodically to empty depots.
⚠ Diminishing Returns

Stacking many extractors on one hotspot gives diminishing output per extractor after the 4th or 5th. For maximum yield, build a second base on another hotspot rather than stacking 10+ extractors on one spot. You'll get more total output across two smaller farms.

Refiners & Production

Refiners convert raw materials into more valuable or useful resources. Your base can house multiple refiners for parallel processing.

RefinerInput SlotsSpeedPlacement
Portable Refiner 1 input Slowest Anywhere (inventory item, deploy like a beacon)
Medium Refiner 2 inputs Medium Base building (requires power)
Large Refiner 3 inputs Fastest Base building (requires power)

Medium and Large Refiners unlock recipes that the Portable can't access — many 2- and 3-ingredient recipes produce far more valuable outputs. These are the workhorses of endgame crafting loops.

📖 Full Refiner Recipe Reference

For a complete breakdown of refining recipes, crafting chains, and the most efficient paths to high-value items like Stasis Devices and Fusion Ignitors, see the Trading & Crafting Manual.

Refiner Farm Layout Tips

  • Place Medium and Large Refiners in a row inside a large room. Leave walking space to access each one's interface
  • Build a row of Storage Containers adjacent so you can quickly load inputs and store outputs
  • Wire all refiners to a single power bus for clean cabling
  • Label rooms with signage (Message Module) if your base has multiple production lines

Wiring & Logic Gates

Beyond basic power delivery, NMS has a surprisingly deep electrical logic system. With switches, inverters, and a bit of creative wiring, you can build automated doors, day/night lights, alarm systems, and even binary counters.

Switches

🔘 Manual Switch Player-Activated

A wall-mounted toggle. Click to turn power on/off. The simplest switch — wire between a power source and device to make it togglable.

📡 Proximity Switch Automatic / Range

Activates when a player enters its detection radius. Outputs a control signal while the player is near. Turns off when they leave. The core component for automatic systems.

🦶 Floor Switch Pressure Pad

A floor-mounted pressure plate. Activates when stepped on. Same use as Proximity Switch but requires direct contact.

🔄 Auto Switch Control = Pass

When control signal is ON, power passes through. When control is OFF, power is blocked. The opposite of the Power Inverter.

The Power Inverter

Power Inverter — How It Works

The Power Inverter has three connections: a green port, a red port, and a control port on top.

  • The green and red ports are interchangeable as power input/output. Convention: use red as input (power in) and green as output (power out) so your wiring stays readable
  • The control port (top) accepts a signal from a switch or other logic device
  • When the control signal is ON → power is BLOCKED (cannot pass through)
  • When the control signal is OFF → power PASSES through normally

This is the opposite of the Auto Switch (which passes power when control is ON).

🧠 Why The Inverter Matters

The Power Inverter is the key to every possible logic gate. You can use it to build a NAND gate, and NAND gates can build every other logic gate (AND, OR, XOR, NOT, NOR, XNOR). The combination of the Inverter and the Auto Switch lets you shortcut many of these constructions.

Example: Automatic Door Opener

Powered doors in NMS are closed when powered. So to make doors that open automatically when you approach, you need to cut the power when the player is near.

Wiring Diagram — Auto Door
Power Source → Inverter (red IN) → Inverter (green OUT) → Door
Power Source → Proximity Switch (IN) → Proximity Switch (OUT) → Inverter (CONTROL)
1
Wire your power source into the Inverter's red (input) port AND into the Proximity Switch's input.
2
Wire the Proximity Switch output into the Inverter's control port (top).
3
Wire the Inverter's green (output) into the door.
4
Result: power flows to the door → door stays closed. Player approaches → Proximity Switch activates → sends signal to Inverter control → Inverter blocks power → door loses power → door opens. Player walks away → switch deactivates → Inverter resumes → door closes.

Other Practical Circuits

💡 Night Lights Auto Switch + Solar

Wire a Solar Panel into an Auto Switch's control. Wire a Battery into the Auto Switch's input and lights into its output. During the day, solar sends control signal → power passes → lights on? No — flip it: wire through an Inverter instead. Solar active → control ON → Inverter blocks → lights OFF during day. At night, solar dies → control OFF → Inverter passes → lights ON.

🚨 Sentinel Alarm Proximity + Lights

Place Proximity Switches at your base perimeter. Wire their output through a Auto Switch to red warning lights and a siren. Anything that triggers the proximity sensor activates the alarm.

🔐 Airlock Sequential Doors

Two doors in sequence, each with its own Proximity Switch and Inverter. The inner door only opens after the outer door closes. Requires careful switch placement so both switches can't trigger simultaneously.

🎵 Sequencer Lights Chained Delays

Chain multiple Auto Switches with Proximity triggers to create sequential lighting patterns — lights that turn on one after another as you walk through a corridor.

Teleporters & Connectivity

Teleporters connect your base to the galactic network and to other points within the same base.

🌀 Base Teleport Module Network Link

Connects your base to the teleporter network. Once built and powered, your base appears in the teleporter list at every Space Station and the Anomaly. Also lets you teleport from the base to any discovered station or other base.

⚡ Short-Range Teleporter Within-Base Travel

A pair of linked pads that teleport you instantly between two points within the same base. Build one at your landing pad area and another at a distant mining outpost for fast travel. Requires power at both ends.

Power Requirement

The Base Teleport Module draws significant power (~20 kP). The Short-Range Teleporter draws less but needs power at both pads. Budget for this when planning your power grid.

Storage & Quality of Life

Storage Containers

There are 10 Storage Containers (numbered 0–9). Each has its own inventory that's shared across all your bases — Container 3 at Base A has the same contents as Container 3 at Base B. Build all 10 in a row at your main base for easy access. They do not require power.

Other Essential Utilities

💾 Save Point Manual Save

Creates a manual save when activated. Build one at every base — essential before risky operations.

🛡 Hazard Protection Unit Shield Recharge

Recharges your hazard protection when you stand near it. Essential on extreme weather planets.

❤ Health Station Health Recharge

Restores health when used. Build in your base near the entrance for post-combat recovery.

🛬 Landing Pad Ship Docking

Provides a dedicated ship landing zone. NPC traders will also land on your pad, letting you buy/sell from your base. Build at least one.

📝 Message Module Text Signs

Place text labels around your base. Use for room names, direction arrows, and production line labels in industrial setups.

🔬 Science Terminal NPC Workstation

Hire a Scientist from a Korvax system and assign them here for research missions and unique blueprints.

Specialist Builds

Underwater Bases

Build beneath the ocean using specialised underwater parts: Underwater Corridors, Viewing Domes, Marine Shelters, and the Nautilon Chamber (for submarine docking). Underwater parts provide oxygen and keep water out. Glass panels give gorgeous ocean views. Pair with a Nautilon Geobay nearby for easy sub access.

Freighter Bases

  • Freighters have their own internal base-building grid — separate from planetary bases
  • Freighter rooms do not require power — everything works passively
  • Build specialist terminals, storage containers, refiners, and farming trays on your freighter for a mobile production hub
  • Freighter base layout uses a fixed room-grid system with corridors connecting sections
  • Part limit is separate from planetary bases

Farming Bases

Build Biodomes or use Planting Trays to grow crops. Each crop has different growth times and can be harvested repeatedly. Key farming crops include:

Pro Tips & Tricks

💡 Extending Base Radius

The default 300u radius extends every time you build at the edge. Walk to the boundary, place a part, and your radius expands to include it. Repeat to push the border out to ~1,000u. This lets you connect a mineral hotspot to an EM hotspot that are hundreds of units apart — just build a trail of parts between them.

💡 Glitch Building

An advanced technique: start placing one object type, then quickly swap to another while the placement hologram is active. The second object inherits the first's snap point, letting you place things in positions the game normally wouldn't allow — floating structures, intersecting parts, and impossible angles. Search "NMS glitch building" for video tutorials.

💡 Free Placement Mode

Toggle Free Placement in the build menu to detach objects from the snap grid. Essential for placing decorations, lights, and furniture exactly where you want them. Also works for structural parts — useful for organic, non-grid designs.

💡 Base Part Limit

Each base has a part limit of approximately 3,000 parts. Complex builds with lots of wiring and small decorations eat into this fast. For mega-builds, use fewer larger parts (large prefab rooms count as 1 part each, same as a single wall panel). Monitor the build count in the Base Computer menu.

💡 Wire Hiding

Use Electrical Cloaking Units to hide ugly wire runs. Place them inside walls or under floors — they relay power without visible wires. Also run wires through floors and behind walls where possible. A clean base has no visible wiring.

💡 Upload Your Base

Use the Base Computer to upload your base. This makes it visible to other players in the same system. Uploaded bases persist even when you're offline. If you DON'T upload, other players won't see your base and it can't be found via the Anomaly's Featured Bases system.

💡 Build on Paradise Planets

For a stress-free main base, find a Paradise or Blissful planet. These have no storms, no extreme hazards, and no aggressive sentinels. Your base will never be interrupted by weather and you won't drain suit power just standing around. Save the hazardous planets for mining outposts.

💡 Terrain Editing Tip

If you flatten terrain with the Terrain Manipulator to create a building pad, the game may eventually regenerate that terrain. To prevent clipping: build foundations first (Wood/Metal/Alloy Floor panels), then build on top. Foundations resist terrain regrowth. Avoid building below the original ground level if possible.

System Overview

Settlements are procedurally generated NPC villages found on planetary surfaces. As Overseer, you manage their growth, resolve disputes, and develop them from struggling outposts into thriving civilizations. A well-run settlement produces valuable resources, units, and rare rewards — and stands as a testament to your leadership across the galaxy.

01

FINDING A SETTLEMENT

Discovery Methods

1
Space Station Mission Board — Look for Settlement missions posted on the Mission Board. These will waypoint you directly to a claimable settlement.
2
Planetary Cartographer — Purchase Settlement Charts from the Cartographer NPC on any Space Station for Navigation Data. These reveal settlement locations on the planet.
3
Signal Scanner — Craft or find a Signal Scanner and scan for Colonial Outposts. Settlements appear as a distinct marker type.
4
Organic Flying Ships — Some settlement-finding missions are offered by NPCs in space. Keep an eye on Space Anomaly notice boards.
🛸
Tip: Each player can only be Overseer of one settlement at a time. If you claim a new one, you forfeit your current settlement. Choose wisely — or intentionally reset to find a better one.

Choosing the Right Settlement

🌍
Planet Type

Avoid extreme hazard worlds (scorched, frozen, toxic) early on. Lush or calm planets make visiting easier.

📊
Starting Stats

Check the Productivity, Happiness, and Sentinel Activity meters. High happiness and low sentinel threat is ideal.

👥
Population

A larger starting population means faster project completion and more production output per cycle.

⚡
Debt Status

Settlements can start in debt. Lower initial debt means fewer resources spent digging out before growth begins.

MODULE 02
02

CLAIMING & FIRST STEPS

1
Arrive at the Settlement — Land nearby and approach the Settlement Overseer's Office terminal (the main console).
2
Interact with the Terminal — Select "Claim Settlement" to become Overseer. If another player owns it, you cannot claim it on a multiplayer server.
3
Review the Dashboard — The Overseer terminal shows Happiness, Productivity, Sentinel Threat, Population, Debt, and daily production. Familiarize yourself with all metrics.
4
Resolve the First Issue — An immediate Issue (dispute or infrastructure problem) is usually presented. Your choices here begin shaping your settlement's character.
5
Start Your First Building Project — Approve a construction project to begin growing your settlement. Prioritize housing to attract more settlers.
⚠ Important Note

You can abandon a settlement to search for a better one — but there's a cooldown timer before you can claim another. Use the time wisely by reviewing what stats you want to optimize before committing.

The Issue System

Every few in-game hours (real-time minutes), an Issue appears at your Overseer terminal. These are the primary way you shape your settlement. There are several types:

Issue Type Description Impact
Settler Dispute Two residents in conflict; you arbitrate Happiness ±
Infrastructure Building or system needs repair/resources Productivity ±
Sentinel Alert Threat level rising; decide response Sentinel Level ±
Resource Crisis Shortage of materials; contribute or ration Productivity & Happiness
New Arrival Migrant wants to join; approve or reject Population +
Trade Request External trade deal proposed Units or Resources
💡
Issue Timer: Issues don't disappear; they wait for you. But the settlement won't advance without you resolving them. Visit regularly for fastest growth.

Managing Debt

Settlements start with debt that must be repaid. Daily income from production is automatically applied to debt reduction. You can also:

  • Personally contribute Units at the Overseer terminal to pay down debt faster
  • Approve high-productivity buildings to increase daily income
  • Avoid resource-heavy decisions until debt is cleared
  • Build Trade Terminals early to boost the unit income multiplier

BUILDINGS & CONSTRUCTION

Construction projects are offered periodically via the Overseer terminal. You choose whether to approve or reject each one. Once approved, residents build it over time (sped up by population size).

Building Categories

🏠
Housing

Increases population cap. More residents = faster construction and higher productivity. Always prioritize early.

🏭
Industry

Boosts daily resource production. Specific types yield different materials (metals, gases, organic). Key for income.

🛡
Defense

Reduces Sentinel threat levels and protects against attacks. Essential if your planet has high sentinel activity.

🌿
Amenities

Gardens, plazas, and community centers boost Happiness significantly. Happy settlers work harder and don't leave.

📡
Commerce

Trade Depots and Markets increase the unit value of daily production — turning raw output into more credits.

🔬
Science

Research facilities unlock new building types and improve productivity efficiency. Unlocks late-game structures.

Construction Requirements

Each building requires materials that you must supply. Common requirements include:

SENTINEL MANAGEMENT

High sentinel threat is one of the most dangerous ongoing problems for a settlement. Unchecked, it leads to full-scale attacks that damage buildings, kill settlers, and crater happiness.

Sentinel Threat Levels

LevelStatusEffect
LowCalm — no patrols near settlementNo negative effects
ModeratePatrols nearby — occasional alertsMild happiness reduction
HighActive threat — attacks possibleSettler morale drops; buildings damaged
CriticalFull assault — sentinels raid settlementSevere damage; population may flee

Reducing Sentinel Threat

  • Build Defense Structures (turrets, shields) through the construction system
  • Choose diplomatic Issue options that don't escalate sentinel attention
  • Avoid mining illegally or harvesting protected resources near the settlement
  • Use Sentinel Bribe options in Issue decisions to temporarily lower threat
  • Install a Sentinel Jammer building once it's available
⚡ Attack Response

When your settlement is under attack, physically travel there and defend it. Fighting off sentinel waves yourself dramatically speeds up the end of the attack and prevents the worst building damage.

06

PRODUCTION & REWARDS

Every real-time day (or when you visit), your settlement produces a batch of resources and units. These accumulate in the Overseer terminal for you to collect.

Production Outputs

💰
Units

Direct credit income. Scales with Productivity rating and Commerce buildings. Can reach millions/day when maxed.

🔩
Raw Materials

Ferrite, Carbon, Oxygen, metals, and more. Useful for construction or selling. Type depends on planet biome.

⚗
Processed Goods

Refined alloys, gases, and compounds. Higher value than raw materials. Requires Industry buildings to unlock.

🌟
Rare Rewards

Settlement milestones yield Trophies, unique decoration items, Nanites, and occasionally Atlantid Multi-Tools.

Maximizing Production

  • Happiness directly multiplies productivity output — keep settlers happy above all else
  • Each Industry building adds a specific resource type and quantity per day
  • Trade buildings multiply the unit value of your resource output
  • The debt counter stops a portion of income — clear it fast for full production benefit
  • Larger population = faster project completion = more buildings = more production
📅
Collection: Production caps out after accumulating ~3 days of output. Visit every few days to collect and prevent waste. The timer resets on each visit.

SETTLERS & RESIDENTS

Each settler is a named NPC with a role, personality, and potential grievances. Residents with specific roles contribute to the settlement's specialization.

Settler Roles

RoleBenefit
🔨 EngineerSpeeds up construction project completion
⚒ MinerBoosts raw material production output
🌾 FarmerIncreases organic and food production
📈 MerchantIncreases unit income and trade bonuses
🛡 GuardReduces sentinel threat growth rate
🔬 ScientistUnlocks advanced research buildings faster

Keeping Settlers Happy

  • Build Amenity structures (gardens, plazas, entertainment)
  • Resolve disputes fairly — check what outcome benefits the most settlers
  • Maintain low sentinel threat — constant raids destroy morale
  • Keep debt low; settlers notice financial strain and become unhappy
  • Approve housing projects quickly to avoid overcrowding
Special Settlers

Occasionally a notable NPC (a traveller, rogue scientist, or alien dignitary) will request to join your settlement. Always approve these — they often bring permanent productivity bonuses or unlock unique building types.

08

PRO TIPS & STRATEGIES

🚀
Teleport Network: Build a Teleporter or Landing Pad in your settlement and save it as a teleport destination. You can then warp back instantly from any Space Station to collect production and resolve issues.
🏆
Settlement Milestones: There are hidden milestone rewards for growing your population and unlocking building tiers. These include trophy decorations and unique Multi-Tool blueprints. Push population to 10, 20, then 50.
⚖
Issue Patterns: Some issues are repeated. Learn which options consistently improve happiness vs. productivity. Write down what works — the same NPC names and situations recur.
⚠
Don't neglect it: Leaving your settlement alone for long periods (especially with high sentinel threat) can result in significant damage. Set a real-life reminder to check in every 2–3 days at minimum.
💎
Hauler Landing Pad: Build a Hauler Landing Pad to attract large freighter-class ships to your settlement. These can be purchased at a significant discount versus buying from a space station.
🌱
Happiness First: In the early game, always prioritize Happiness over Productivity. A happy settlement is self-sustaining. An unhappy but productive one will collapse as settlers leave.
🗺
Planet Selection: If you haven't claimed a settlement yet — choose a planet with valuable resources (Activated Indium, Gold, etc.) so your production output has high sell value at the trade terminal.

Recommended Build Order

1
Housing × 2–3 — Expand population cap first. More settlers = everything else happens faster.
2
Amenity × 1 — Keep happiness high early so settlers don't abandon the settlement.
3
Industry × 2 — Start generating daily resources to pay off debt and begin accumulation.
4
Defense × 1 — Protect early infrastructure from sentinel raids once threat starts building.
5
Commerce × 1 — Trade Depot to increase the unit value of your daily output significantly.
6
Repeat — Cycle back through housing, industry, and amenities. Scale toward Science buildings for advanced options.

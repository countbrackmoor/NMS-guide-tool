System Overview

Settlements are procedurally generated NPC villages found on planetary surfaces. As Overseer, you manage their growth, resolve disputes, and develop them from struggling outposts into thriving civilizations. A well-run settlement produces valuable resources, units, and rare rewards — and stands as a testament to your leadership across the galaxy.

01

FINDING A SETTLEMENT

Discovery Methods

1
Space Station Mission Board — Look for Settlement missions posted on the Mission Board. These will waypoint you directly to a claimable settlement.
2
Planetary Cartographer — Purchase Settlement Charts from the Cartographer NPC on any Space Station for Navigation Data. These reveal settlement locations on the planet.
3
Signal Scanner — Craft or find a Signal Scanner and scan for Colonial Outposts. Settlements appear as a distinct marker type.
4
Distress Signal — While flying in space, you may receive an automatic transmission about a settlement under attack. Defend it from Sentinels and you'll have first claim on the Settlement Hub Interface.
🛸
Tip: As of the Beacon update (2025), you can be Overseer of up to four settlements simultaneously. Each has its own management mission in your log. You can also formally retire as Overseer of any settlement.

Choosing the Right Settlement

🌍
Planet Type

Avoid extreme hazard worlds (scorched, frozen, toxic) early on. Lush or calm planets make visiting easier.

📊
Starting Stats

Check Productivity vs. Maintenance Cost, Happiness, and Sentinel Alert Level. Aim for low debt and a Sentinel Alert at "Low".

👥
Population

A larger starting population means faster project completion and more production output per cycle.

⚡
Debt Status

Settlements can start in debt. Lower initial debt means fewer resources spent digging out before growth begins.

02

CLAIMING & FIRST STEPS

1
Arrive at the Settlement — Land nearby and approach the Settlement Hub Interface — a statue-like console at the center of the settlement.
2
Apply to be Overseer — Submit a faction-specific offering: 3 Vy'keen Daggers, 3 GekNip, or 3 Convergence Cubes depending on the dominant species.
3
Build the Overseer's Office — A marker points to the construction site. Supply 210 Silicate Powder or Ferrite Dust, then 5 Metal Plating, then 3 Microprocessors. Each stage has a wait timer.
4
Use the Administration Terminal — Inside the completed office. Shows Happiness, Productivity, Maintenance Cost, Sentinel Alert Level, Population, and the debt status.
5
Resolve the First Decision — A construction opportunity, citizen request, or dispute will be ready. Your choices shape stats from the very start.
⚠ Important Note

You can now manage up to four settlements simultaneously (Beacon update). To drop one, formally retire as its Overseer via the Settlement Hub Interface — your progress at that settlement is lost, but you can then claim a new one. Make sure no construction project is in progress when claiming a second+ settlement, or the new Overseer's Office build may be bugged.

Productivity must exceed Maintenance Cost for the settlement to produce goods. If Maintenance is higher, the settlement falls into debt and production halts until it's repaid.

The Decision System

Periodically, decisions appear at your Administration Terminal. These are the primary way you shape your settlement. Common types include:

Decision Type Description Impact
Citizen Dispute Two residents in conflict; you arbitrate Happiness ±
Construction Opportunity Choose between two proposed buildings Productivity / Happiness
Sentinel Alert Threat level rising; decide response Sentinel Alert ±
Policy Decision Settlement-wide rule change proposal Productivity & Happiness
New Arrival Migrant wants to join; approve or reject Population +
Citizen Request Settler asks for a resource or favor Happiness ±
💡
Decision Timer: Decisions don't disappear — they wait for you at the terminal. Construction stages each take 1–2 hours of real time, so settlements grow steadily over days, not minutes.

Managing Debt

Most settlements start with debt and growing maintenance costs. Production output is automatically applied to debt reduction before any tribute is generated for the Overseer. To recover faster:

  • Personally pay off debt by contributing Units at the Administration Terminal
  • Approve buildings that boost productivity faster than they add maintenance cost
  • Upgrade existing buildings — higher classes (toward S) give better productivity-to-cost ratios
  • Overpaying debt with units now adds to the settlement's reserves, producing bonus output equivalent to the excess (Beacon update)

BUILDINGS & CONSTRUCTION

Construction projects are offered periodically as decisions at the Administration Terminal. New buildings start at C-class and can be upgraded to higher classes (B, A, S) over time, improving their productivity and reducing their maintenance cost. Higher-class buildings unlock more lucrative production options as Overseer.

Building Categories

🏠
Housing

Increases population cap. More residents = faster construction and higher productivity. Always prioritize early.

🏭
Industry

Boosts daily resource production. Specific types yield different materials (metals, gases, organic). Key for income.

🛡
Defense

Reduces Sentinel threat levels and protects against attacks. Essential if your planet has high sentinel activity.

🌿
Amenities

Gardens, plazas, and community centers boost Happiness significantly. Happy settlers work harder and don't leave.

📡
Commerce

Trade Depots and Markets increase the unit value of daily production — turning raw output into more credits.

🔬
Science

Research facilities unlock new building types and improve productivity efficiency. Unlocks late-game structures.

Construction Requirements

SENTINEL MANAGEMENT

A high Sentinel Alert Level is one of the most dangerous ongoing problems for a settlement. Unchecked, it leads to full-scale attacks that damage buildings and crater happiness.

Sentinel Alert Levels

LevelStatusEffect
LowCalm — no patrols near settlementNo negative effects
ModeratePatrols nearby — occasional alertsMild happiness reduction
HighActive threat — attacks possibleSettler morale drops; buildings damaged
CriticalFull assault — sentinels raid settlementSevere damage; population may flee

Reducing the Alert Level

  • Approve Defense-related buildings when offered as construction opportunities
  • Choose decision options that don't escalate sentinel attention
  • Avoid clearing wildlife or attacking Sentinels close to the settlement boundary
  • Some special features and policies reduce Sentinel alert over time once unlocked
  • Autophage settlements always start with a specialist feature that reduces Sentinel attention
⚡ Attack Response

When your settlement is under attack, physically travel there and defend it. Fighting off sentinel waves yourself dramatically speeds up the end of the attack and prevents the worst building damage.

PRODUCTION & REWARDS

Every real-time day (or when you visit), your settlement produces a batch of resources and units. These accumulate in the Overseer terminal for you to collect.

Production Outputs

💰
Units

Direct credit income. Scales with Productivity rating and Commerce buildings. Can reach millions/day when maxed.

🔩
Raw Materials

Ferrite, Carbon, Oxygen, metals, and more. Useful for construction or selling. Type depends on planet biome.

⚗
Processed Goods

Refined alloys, gases, and compounds. Higher value than raw materials. Requires Industry buildings to unlock.

🌟
Rare Rewards

Settlement milestones yield Trophies, unique decoration items, Nanites, and occasionally Atlantid Multi-Tools.

Maximizing Production

  • Happiness directly multiplies productivity output — keep settlers happy above all else
  • Each Industry building adds a specific resource type and quantity per day
  • Trade buildings multiply the unit value of your resource output
  • The debt counter stops a portion of income — clear it fast for full production benefit
  • Larger population = faster project completion = more buildings = more production
📅
Collection Timing: Each output hopper shows a production timer for when the next batch is ready. Collecting goods resets the timer — so collecting early can waste the current partial cycle. Wait until the timer completes before collecting to maximize output. If the settlement falls into debt, any goods in the hoppers are cleared and production pauses until the debt is resolved.

SETTLERS & RESIDENTS

Settlements have a population of generic NPCs that grow over time as you approve New Arrival decisions. Citizens occasionally raise requests or get into disputes — these are the primary way they influence stats. Settlements also have special visitors with unique gifts and dialogue.

Citizen Decisions

DecisionEffect
📋 Citizen RequestSettler asks for resources or a favor — answering affects Happiness
⚖ Citizen DisputeTwo settlers in conflict — your verdict shifts Happiness for both
📜 Policy DecisionA proposed settlement-wide rule with productivity vs happiness trade-offs
🚪 New ArrivalMigrant wants to join — increases population if approved
🎁 Visitor EncounterAn outsider arrives with gifts or trades for specific resources

Keeping Settlers Happy

  • Build Amenity structures (gardens, plazas, entertainment)
  • Resolve disputes fairly — check what outcome benefits the most settlers
  • Maintain low sentinel threat — constant raids destroy morale
  • Keep debt low; settlers notice financial strain and become unhappy
  • Approve housing projects quickly to avoid overcrowding
Special Settlers

Occasionally a notable NPC (a traveller, rogue scientist, or alien dignitary) will request to join your settlement. Always approve these — they often bring permanent productivity bonuses or unlock unique building types.

08

PRO TIPS & STRATEGIES

🚀
Teleport Network: Build a Teleporter or Landing Pad in your settlement and save it as a teleport destination. You can then warp back instantly from any Space Station to collect production and resolve issues.
🏆
Settlement Milestones: There are hidden milestone rewards for growing your population and unlocking building tiers. These include trophy decorations and unique Multi-Tool blueprints. Push population to 10, 20, then 50.
⚖
Issue Patterns: Some issues are repeated. Learn which options consistently improve happiness vs. productivity. Write down what works — the same NPC names and situations recur.
⚠
Don't neglect it: Leaving your settlement alone for long periods (especially with a high Sentinel Alert) can result in significant damage. Construction stages take 1–2 hours each, so steady visits every day or two keep your queue moving.
💎
Landing Pad Salvage: Landing Pad buildings at settlements (post-Beacon update) provide utility for locating crashed ships nearby and salvaging starships that land at the settlement.
🤖
Autophage Settlements: Rare settlements run by Autophage robots can be found on inhabited planets. They start with a built-in feature that reduces Sentinel attention. Travellers who've completed "They Who Returned" may receive an invitation, or purchase Autophage Settlement Charts.
🌱
Happiness First: In the early game, always prioritize Happiness over Productivity. A happy settlement is self-sustaining. An unhappy but productive one will collapse as settlers leave.
🗺
Planet Selection: If you haven't claimed a settlement yet — pick a calm, hospitable planet so visits aren't a slog. The settlement produces its own item types based on race and buildings rather than mining surrounding resources, so the planet's resource map matters less than its weather and terrain.

Recommended Build Order

1
Housing × 2–3 — Expand population cap first. More settlers = everything else happens faster.
2
Amenity × 1 — Keep happiness high early so settlers don't abandon the settlement.
3
Industry × 2 — Start generating daily resources to pay off debt and begin accumulation.
4
Defense × 1 — Protect early infrastructure from sentinel raids once threat starts building.
5
Commerce × 1 — Trade Depot to increase the unit value of your daily output significantly.
6
Repeat — Cycle back through housing, industry, and amenities. Scale toward Science buildings for advanced options.

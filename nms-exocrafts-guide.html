Section 01

Exocrafts

Exocrafts are ground and aquatic vehicles you can summon on planetary surfaces. Each fills a different role — from fast scouting to deep mining to underwater exploration. They're invaluable for efficient planet traversal and bulk resource gathering.

Unlocking Exocrafts

All Exocraft Geobay blueprints can be purchased at the Construction Research Station aboard the Space Anomaly for Salvaged Data (~4–10 each). Alternatively, hire an Exocraft Technician at your base from a Space Station and complete their mission line to unlock blueprints sequentially.

Summoning

Built Exocrafts are summoned at their Geobay on a planet. To summon anywhere on a planet, build an Exocraft Summoning Station at a base in the same system. To summon any Exocraft anywhere on any planet without a Geobay at all, install the Orbital Exocraft Materialiser technology on your freighter.

Exocraft Upgrades

Each Exocraft has its own technology grid for upgrades. Modules are specific to each vehicle — a Colossus upgrade won't fit a Roamer. Buy or craft them and install using the same adjacency bonus rules as ships.

Universal Upgrade Priorities

⛏ Mining Laser All Exocrafts

Install and upgrade on any mining-capable Exocraft. Dramatically faster than the Multi-Tool for large deposits.

🔋 Exocraft Acceleration Engine Upgrade

Boosts top speed and boost duration. Priority on Roamer and Pilgrim for traversal efficiency.

📡 Survey Device Scanner

Reveals buried resource deposits in a radius around the Exocraft. Essential for efficient mining runs.

🛡 Exocraft Shield Defense

Adds shield capacity. Priority for Minotaur to make it a proper combat platform.

🔫 Mounted Cannon Weapons

Vehicle-mounted weapons for Exocrafts. Upgrade for Minotaur to turn it into a sentinel-killing machine.

🧲 Cargo Expansion Colossus Only

Increases Colossus inventory capacity. Stack these — the Colossus's purpose is cargo, so maximize it.

Orbital Exocraft Materialiser

Install the Orbital Exocraft Materialiser technology on your freighter and you can summon any Exocraft anywhere on any planet — no Geobay or Summoning Station required. One of the best quality-of-life upgrades for players who use Exocrafts regularly. Purchase the blueprint from Iteration: Hyperion on the Anomaly.

Pro Tips & Secrets

💡 Own Multiple Ships

You can own up to 12 ships simultaneously. Keep a dedicated Fighter for combat, an Explorer for long warps, a Hauler for trading runs, and your favorite for daily use. Swap between them via the Quick Menu anywhere.

💡 Exotic Ships — Always S-Class

Exotic ships are always S-class with no exceptions, and always have a unique visual design that appears nowhere else. They're smaller than standard ships but their stats punch above their size. If you see an Exotic land, buy it immediately — it may never return.

💡 Coordinate Communities

Websites like nmssaves.com and subreddits like r/NMSCoordinateExchange list exact portal coordinates for rare ship spawns, including specific Exotic designs, rare Living Ship variants, and S-class Haulers at known Trade Posts. Use these to skip hundreds of hours of searching.

💡 Dismantle for Nanites

When you upgrade a ship or tool module to a higher class, dismantle the old module — don't sell it. Dismantling gives you Nanite Clusters at a much better rate than selling, especially for A and S-class modules.

💡 Buying Ships from Freighters

Freighters have their own landing pads where NPC ships periodically dock. Hang out on a friendly freighter's hangar deck and check arriving ships the same way you would at a Space Station — sometimes you'll find a class or design you can't get elsewhere, including rare S-class haulers.

💡 Sentinel Interceptor Reroll

Before approaching an Interceptor wreck, save manually. Interact with the wreck to check its class. Reload if not S-class, and the wreck rerolls. The Hyaline Brain quest and Monolith trip only need to be done once per crash site — subsequent reloads keep the same Brain, so you can pop in and check stats fast.

💡 Colossus Mining Loop

For maximum bulk yield: summon Colossus → drive it onto an Activated Indium or other ore deposit → use the built-in Mining Laser to harvest at speed → its 40+ slot cargo holds an enormous stack → land your starship nearby, swap to it, and transfer the stack via Matter Beam straight to your freighter. Repeat. Fastest bulk gathering loop in the game.
